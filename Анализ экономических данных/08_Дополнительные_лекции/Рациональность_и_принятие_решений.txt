Warm up

The concept of rationality plays an important role in Economics
Rationality is defined in a very particular way in Consumer Choice Theory
If certain assumptions about consumersС preferences are satisfied,
then such consumers are considered to be rational
Rationality in Economics

Let us consider some bundles of goods
(imagine carts in a supermarket with the same overall cost)
I. Completeness
Consumers can determine whether:
[A preferred to B], or
[B is preferred to A], or
[there is an indifference between A and B]
In other words, consumers can compare bundles
Consumer Choice

What about VS?
II. Transitivity
The transitivity assumption states that coffee must be preferred to tea
Why is this assumption important?
Allows to establish the order of options (alternatives):
what is better, what is worse
Consumer Choice

And this is it for rationality!
As long as consumersС preferences satisfy completeness and transitivity,
such consumers are considered to be rational
More generally, the concept of preferences is a cornerstone of Microeconomics
Note that:
(1) we did not assume anything specific about bundles
(2) we did not even introduce money (pure preferences)
Consumer Choice

One more important assumption
III. Continuity
Continuity = no discontinuity in preferences
Discontinuity appears when a consumer cares only about one good
(and prefers even the smallest amounts to everything else)
This last assumption gives economists the ability to construct models
However, it does NOT imply that economists assume people
to actually have mathematical model in their minds!
Consumer Choice

A key notion discussed around rationality is selfishness
Moreover, these two terms are often used interchangeably
However, this is not so simple
Such new areas of economics as Behavioral Economics and
Experimental Economics investigate selfishness
Selfishness

Let us imagine that one bundle now contains some books,
which are going to be donated to the library (if the bundle is chosen)
Does it necessarily make such bundle worse?
Can a rational consumer pick such bundle?
If yes, does it break the rationality-selfishness link?
Modern economics has no problem with that
Selfishness

If you prefer to care about others, then this is valuable to you
Thus, doing it is both selfish and rational
Incurring costs (money, time, effort) to help others is not a problem
Volunteering should not be treated differently than buying an apple
(as long as you enjoy both)
