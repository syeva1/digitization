Biased attention
This is when we selectively focus on information that confirms our views while ignoring or discounting data that doesn't.

Biased interpretation
This is when we consciously interpret information in a way that confirms our beliefs.

HOW TO AVOID CONFIRMATION BIAS?

ADMIT THE EXISTENCE OF CONFIRMATION BIAS.
LOOK AT OTHER POINTS OF VIEW AND LISTEN TO THE OTHER SIDE OF THE ARGUMENT WITH AN OPEN MIND.
SURROUND OURSELVES WITH PEOPLE WHOSE OPINIONS DIFFER FROM OURSELVES TO CHALLENGE EXISTING IDEAS.
ACKNOWLEDGE THAT WE DO NOT KNOW EVERYTHING AND THAT NEW INFORMATION CAN HELP US UNDERSTAND THE WORLD.
UNDERSTAND THAT OUR VIEWS SHOULD AND COULD CHANGE OVER TIME DUE TO LIMITED INFORMATION.
BE OPEN TO CHANGE AND DIFFERENT IDEAS TO AVOID CONFIRMATION BIAS.

OUR IDEAS!
INVESTMENT DECISIONS

AN INVESTOR WHO IS OPTIMISTIC ABOUT A PARTICULAR COMPANY MAY LOOK FOR INFORMATION CONFIRMING HIS POSITIVE OUTLOOK, IGNORING THE WARNING SIGNS.
THIS BIAS MAY LEAD TO THE INABILITY TO CAREFULLY EVALUATE ALL AVAILABLE INFORMATION AND MAKE INFORMED INVESTMENT DECISIONS.

EMPLOYEE PERFORMANCE ASSESSMENT

CONFIRMATION BIAS CAN ALSO AFFECT WORKPLACE PERFORMANCE EVALUATION.
IF A MANAGER HAS A PRECONCEIVED IDEA ABOUT THE ABILITIES OR WORK ETHIC OF A PARTICULAR EMPLOYEE, HE CAN SELECTIVELY INTERPRET OR MEMORIZE EXAMPLES CONFIRMING HIS BELIEFS, IGNORING EVIDENCE TO THE CONTRARY.
THIS CAN LEAD TO BIASED ASSESSMENTS AND POTENTIALLY UNFAIR TREATMENT OF EMPLOYEES.

BBA

OUR IDEAS!
MARKETING AND ADVERTISING.

COMPANIES OFTEN RELY ON CONFIRMATION BIAS TO INFLUENCE CONSUMER PERCEPTIONS AND PURCHASE DECISIONS.
FOR EXAMPLE, A COMPANY MAY SELECTIVELY PROVIDE POSITIVE FEEDBACK OR CUSTOMER REVIEWS TO ENHANCE THE PERCEPTION OF PRODUCT QUALITY.
THIS CAN CREATE A BIASED IMPRESSION AND INFLUENCE THE CHOICE OF CONSUMERS.

COMPETITOR ANALYSIS

CONFIRMATION BIAS CAN BE A PITFALL WHEN CONDUCTING COMPETITOR ANALYSIS.
IF A BUSINESS HAS A PRE-EXISTING PERCEPTION THAT THEIR COMPETITORS ARE INFERIOR IN CERTAIN ASPECTS, THEY MAY ACTIVELY SEARCH FOR INFORMATION THAT REINFORCES THIS BELIEF WHILE DISREGARDING OR UNDERESTIMATING EVIDENCE OF THEIR COMPETITORS' STRENGTHS.
THIS BIAS CAN LEAD TO A SKEWED UNDERSTANDING OF THE COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE AND HINDER EFFECTIVE STRATEGIC DECISION-MAKING.
