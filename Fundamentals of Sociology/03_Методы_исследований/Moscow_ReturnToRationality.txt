} WT Nelson Hairston, Frederick Smith and Lawrence Slobodkin

(HSS) proposed in 1960 that vegetation patterns were caused by food
consumption patterns by herbivores. They proposed the Green World
Hypothesis, that the world is green because predators limit herbivore
populations and the amount of vegetation they consume. This
hypothesis was tested by Robert Paine, who observed the carnivorous
sea star Pisaster ochraceous eat Mytilus californianus, a mussel. He
tested to see what would happen if he removed the carnivorous sea
star. After one year, he saw that where the sea star had been removed,
the mussels dominated, consuming all the vegetation and forcing out
other species. After one year, seven of fifteen species he had noted at
the start of his experiment were gone.Ф
