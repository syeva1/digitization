Fundamentals of Sociology

1. Course description
Sociology is the study of human societies to include human behaviors and social interactions with the acknowledgement that cultural norms are related to actions. Areas of focus within sociology include social organization, demography, social psychology, social change, human ecology, and applied areas such as criminology, which are studied through a variety of empirical assessments and analyses.
Topics within the course include basic concepts such as social fact and social action, the statistical study of human populations, personality assessments and interpersonal relationships, stratification of societies as related to race, gender, and social class, and the study of the interactions between human and non-human nature in different cultures.
To explore these topics, students will engage in activities (inside and outside the classroom) to ensure an understanding of the course topics and the applications of such concepts in both theoretical and applied research.
2. Course goals
This course is designed to provide an overview of fundamental concepts and methods for understanding human behaviors and interactions from a sociological perspective.

5. Types of coursework
During the course students work individually and in groups.
 Group work: group work as a teaching strategy requires students to engage in learning activities within the same group over a period while working on a substantial task with a shared outcome (such as a report or a project). Research benefits from a person's ability to work cooperatively as well as their ability to work independently. During the course group work is used in small discussion groups in lectures and exercises.
 Individual work: individual work helps students to develop the capacity to learn without always leaning on others. This can hopefully lead to some sense of learner autonomy, which is a critical goal of the program. As students rely on their own resources it strengthens them in discerning information and learning.

Examination
If the student received ТunsatisfactoryУ grade = fail after the course, the student is obliged to take a written exam. The examination will consist of a 90-minute written test that includes the identification of ten quotations from required course readings and a written essay. The use of any electronic devices or any written materials is prohibited. The student must:
1. Attribute the given quotations; identify the sections of the texts (such as a chapter) where the quotations are taken from as well as their authors, titles, and year of publication.
2. Based on the given quotations, write an essay in English (1000 words) analyzing semantic relationships between the texts where these quotations are taken from.
